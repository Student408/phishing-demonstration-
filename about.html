Stay Safe from Phishing Attacks

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers attempt to trick you into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or other personal data. This is often done through fake emails, messages, or websites that look legitimate.

How to Recognize a Phishing Attempt

Here are some common signs of phishing:

  • Suspicious Emails or Messages: Be cautious of emails or messages from unknown senders, especially those asking for personal information or urging you to click on a link.
  • Urgent or Threatening Language: Phishing messages often use scare tactics to pressure you into taking immediate action.
  • Unexpected Attachments: Be wary of attachments from unknown sources, as they could contain malware.
  • Mismatched URLs: Always hover over links to check the actual URL before clicking. Phishing sites often mimic legitimate websites but have slightly different URLs.

How to Protect Yourself

Follow these tips to avoid falling victim to phishing:

  • Verify the Source: Always double-check the sender's email address and the URL of any website before providing personal information.
  • Use Security Software: Ensure your computer and devices are protected with up-to-date security software.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication: Add an extra layer of security to your accounts by enabling two-factor authentication (2FA) where possible.
  • Stay Informed: Educate yourself about the latest phishing techniques and be cautious of any unusual requests.

What to Do If You Suspect a Phishing Attack

If you think you've received a phishing message or visited a phishing website, do not provide any information. Report the incident to your IT department or the relevant authorities.
